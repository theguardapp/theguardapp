Privacy Policy & Terms of Use

Plain language. No surprises. The Guard is local-first. These documents simply describe what that means in practice.

Last updated: January 2026. This policy covers The Guard mobile application and the website at theguardapp.github.io.

Privacy Policy

1. Summary in one paragraph

The Guard is an Android application that runs entirely on your device. We do not operate any backend service that receives, stores, or processes data from the app. We have no analytics SDKs, no advertising SDKs, no crash-reporting service, and no telemetry of any kind. The app needs no account, and we never ask for one.

2. What the app reads on your device

To deliver its features, The Guard reads information from your device locally. Depending on which modules you enable, this can include:

  • Process and package metadata (running apps, installed apps, permissions held).
  • System sensors (CPU and battery temperature, charge level, charge cycles).
  • Storage metadata and, when you initiate a forensic scan, raw block-level data from internal storage or an attached OTG device.
  • Network status indicators (connection type, signal level, but never traffic content).

All of this stays on the device. It is processed in-memory or written to the app's private storage area, which is sandboxed by Android and inaccessible to other apps.

3. What we collect (nothing)

We do not collect, transmit, or otherwise receive any of the data the app reads. There is no remote server we operate that the app talks to. You can verify this by inspecting your device's network traffic while the app is running; you will see no outbound connections to our infrastructure, because we don't have any.

4. Permissions explained

Android requires us to request certain permissions to provide functionality. We only request what is strictly necessary, and we explain each one in-app before it is used:

  • Storage / Media access: to read storage you explicitly select for forensic recovery.
  • USB host access: to read OTG-attached devices when you connect one for analysis.
  • Usage stats: to power performance and battery analytics.
  • Notifications: to surface security and thermal alerts.

You can revoke any permission at any time via Android system settings; the affected feature will simply stop working until the permission is restored.

5. Sharing and exporting data

You may export reports, recovered files, or scan results from the app. When you do, you choose where they go (local storage, a connected USB drive, a sharing target you select). The Guard's default share chooser is filtered to exclude known cloud-storage providers so that local-only sharing remains the obvious path; you can still choose any installed app explicitly. We never share data on your behalf.

6. Children's privacy

The Guard is a general-purpose utility intended for adults and corporate environments. We do not knowingly target children, and because we do not collect any data, we cannot identify users by age.

7. Changes to this policy

If we ever materially change this policy, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page and, where reasonable, surface a notice in-app on next launch. Because we have no user accounts and no contact list, this page is the canonical source.

8. Contact

For privacy questions write to hello@theguardapp.com. For security issues, please use security@theguardapp.com.
