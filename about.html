Our story

Engineered for those who need certainty

The Guard was built around a simple belief: a tool that monitors your device, reads your storage, and protects your privacy should never demand that you trust a remote server. So we built one that doesn't.

Principles

The pillars we refuse to compromise

Three commitments that shape every architectural decision in the platform.

Local-first by design

Every byte we read, every metric we compute, and every report we generate stays on your device. No telemetry. No analytics SDK. No background uploads. Ever.

Forensic-grade integrity

Acquisition uses a software write-blocker, drive-health surfacing, and cryptographically signed reports, so what you see is exactly what was on the device, and you can prove it.

No dark patterns

No upsells, no nag screens, no features locked behind a subscription. The Guard is feature-complete, free, and respects your time.

Why we built it

The Android ecosystem is full of security apps that, ironically, exfiltrate metadata, fingerprint your hardware, or sell scan results to a third-party SDK. The Guard exists because the people who care most about device security are the people least willing to accept that trade-off.

Our team comes from a background in mobile forensics, embedded systems, and offensive security. We wanted a tool we'd trust on our own phones, one that did the heavy lifting (carving, SMART-style monitoring, permission auditing) without ever phoning home.

How the platform is structured

Internally The Guard is six modules sharing a common storage and reporting layer:

  • Security Monitor: process tree, permission usage, anomaly heuristics.
  • Thermal & Performance: multi-tier alerts, throttling detection, heat history.
  • Battery Intelligence: cycle accounting, capacity curves, drain forensics.
  • Forensic Recovery: signature-based carving over internal and OTG storage with a software write-blocker.
  • Privacy Permissions: timestamped access logs you control, with on-device retention.
  • System Diagnostics: CPU load, RAM pressure, storage integrity, network metrics.

Every module emits structured events to a single local store. Reports are generated, signed, and saved on-device, and never leave it unless you explicitly export them.

What we will never do

  • Add advertising, analytics, or tracking SDKs.
  • Upload device data, scan results, or recovered files to any server.
  • Charge for features that should be standard (write-blocking, signed reports, full module access).
  • Sell, share, or monetize the data your device produces.

Who's behind it

The Guard is built and maintained by a small, independent team of engineers. We are privately funded, closed-source by choice, and answerable only to the people who use the app. If you'd like to reach us — for a partnership, a deployment question, or just to say hello — see the contact section.

One last thing

If you find a security issue, please write to security@theguardapp.com. We take responsible disclosure seriously, and we will always credit researchers who help us improve.
